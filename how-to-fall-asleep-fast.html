How to fall asleep fast - all the best recommendations


If you struggle to fall asleep at night, it can harm every other part of your life.

That's why we compiled a list of everything you can do to start falling asleep more quickly at night.

Here are all of our suggestions, to help you wake up better every day:


🚫 Things to avoid

  • Screens 📵
    Stop using electronic devices for at least an hour before bed. Set an alarm on your phone to remind you when this time has come each evening.
  • Stimulants ☕️
    Experts recommend avoiding caffeine for at least eight hours before you want to fall asleep.
  • Stress 🧘‍♂️
    Try to manage stress and anxiety: practicing meditation or yoga to calm your mind can help, or you may want to consider journaling or talking to a friend or therapist to alleviate worries or concerns.
  • Late or long naps 😴
    If you nap, try to do it early in the day and keep naps between 20-30 minutes.
  • Large meals 🍔
    Heavy meals or spicy foods close to bedtime can cause discomfort and indigestion, making it harder to fall asleep.
  • Lots of liquids 💧
    Reduce your fluid intake in the hours leading up to bedtime to avoid waking up to go to the bathroom during the night.

🛏️ The right environment

  • Only use your bedroom for sleeping 🛌
    Distractions like TV's, laptops or phones can prevent your mind from switching into sleep mode.
  • Obtain high-quality bedding 🛏️
    Your mattress, pillows, and sheets are worthwhile investments for better sleep & health.
  • Keep your bedroom cool ❄️
    A comfortable low temperature is conducive for sleep.
  • Keep light out 💡
    Make your bedroom dark as much as possible, use blackout curtains or eye masks if necessary.
  • Minimize noise 🤫
    Make sure your bedroom is quiet (ear plugs may be necessary), and if you find complete silence unsettling, try using a white noise machine.
  • Don't neglect scent 🕯️
    Use a lavender-scented candle or diffuser to create a soothing ambiance in your bedroom. Done every night, this creates a powerful mental signal.

✅ Good habits

  • Establish and maintain a consistent sleep routine 🔄
    ...By going to bed and waking up at the same time every day, even on weekends.
  • Regular daily exercise or physical activity 🏃
    Either the gym or just a daily walk can help, but avoid vigorous exercise for at least an hour before bedtime.
  • Lower the lights in your home 🔅
    An hour before bedtime, to signal to your body that it's time to wind down and prepare for sleep.
  • Do some calming activities before bed 😌
    Such as practicing deep breathing or listening to soothing music.

🛌 When in bed

  • Read a book 📚
    Preferably fiction with light subject matter, and preferably on an e-Reader so that you can read with the lights off (set the screen brightness as low as you can while still be able to read).
  • Progressive muscle relaxation 💪
    Starting from your toes and moving upward, tense and then relax each muscle group in your body to promote relaxation.
  • Try the "military method" 🪖
    1. Relax your entire face, including the muscles inside your mouth.
    2. Drop your shoulders to release the tension and let your hands drop to the side of your body.
    3. Exhale, relaxing your chest.
    4. Relax your legs, thighs, and calves.
    5. Clear your mind for 10 seconds by imagining a relaxing scene.
    6. If this doesn’t work, try saying the words “don’t think” over and over for 10 seconds.
