How to wake up better, according to the science


We're not here to waste any time, so let's get right to it!

According to scientific research, here are the 5 most important things you can do to wake up better every day:


1️⃣

A consistent schedule is key 🔑


Establish a regular sleep routine by going to bed 🛌 and waking up at the same time every day 🗓️, including weekends.

This helps regulate your body's internal clock and enhances the quality of your sleep.


2️⃣

Prioritize sleep duration ⏱️


Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep 💤 each night.

This duration allows your body to complete essential sleep cycles 🔄, leading to better waking up and improved daytime alertness.


3️⃣

Light exposure is your friend 🌞


Expose yourself to bright natural light in the morning 🌅, as it helps suppress the sleep hormone melatonin and signals your body to wake up.

Open your curtains 🪟, take a walk 🚶 outside, or consider using a light therapy lamp 🛋️ if needed.


4️⃣

Avoid electronics before bed 📵


The blue light emitted by screens can disrupt your sleep 😵‍💫. Minimize your exposure to electronic devices like smartphones, tablets, and TVs 📺 at least an hour before bedtime.

Instead, engage in relaxing activities such as reading a book 📚 or taking a warm bath 🛀.


5️⃣

Create a soothing bedtime routine 🧘


Develop a wind-down routine to prepare your mind and body for sleep.

This may include deep breathing or meditation, calming music 🎶, or journaling 📓 in order to unload your thoughts and worries.
